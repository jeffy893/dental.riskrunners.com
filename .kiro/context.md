# Kiro Context: Risk Runners Dental Captive

## Project Overview
**Site Name:** Risk Runners Dental Captive
**Target Domain:** dental.riskrunners.com
**Mission:** To create a unified risk-management and financial suite for a syndicate of 12 dentists. This captive insurance structure allows dental practitioners to pool risk, smooth cash flow, and retain wealth instead of paying unrecoverable sunk costs to standard commercial malpractice and liability insurers.

## Design & UI/UX Guidelines
* **Mobile-First & Responsive:** The site MUST be highly mobile-friendly, adapting seamlessly from desktop to tablet to mobile. Use flexible grids, responsive padding, and mobile-optimized navigation (e.g., hamburger menus).
* **Aesthetic:** Clean, high-trust, professional, and modern. Use a color palette suitable for dental and financial security (e.g., deep blues, clean whites, subtle teals, and slate grays).
* **Architecture:** Single-page or cleanly routed multi-page structure ready for GitHub Pages deployment (static site compatible).

## Conceptual Mapping (Adapted from Integral Mass Captive)
Use the structural flow of the Integral Mass Captive website, but map it specifically to dental practices:

1.  **The Ecosystem:** * *Instead of GCs:* Practice Owners (Dentists).
    * *Instead of Property Owners:* DSOs (Dental Support Organizations) or Multi-practice syndicates.
    * *Instead of Tenants:* Patients requiring comprehensive, long-term restorative care.
2.  **The Insurance Problem (The "Corporate R&D Analogy"):**
    * Commercial insurance for dentists is a "sunk cost." You pay premiums, and if you have no claims, the insurer keeps the profit.
    * *The Captive Solution:* 12 Dentists form a syndicate. They pay premiums into *their own* captive. This protects their P&L from unexpected setbacks, and if loss experience is good, the dentists share in the dividends.
3.  **Covered Innovation Risks (Fortuitous Only):**
    * Equipment failures (e.g., broken CBCT scanners, 3D printers).
    * Unforeseen complications with new advanced techniques (e.g., novel implant procedures, laser dentistry).
    * Cybersecurity breaches (patient data).
    * *Explicit Exclusion:* Speculative risks (market changes, practice expansion gambles, intentional malpractice) are expressly excluded. Coverage is for fortuitous events only.
4.  **Collective Risk Pooling:**
    * 12 Dentists sharing risk means no single practice bears catastrophic losses alone.
